What are Memes?

Memes are the internet’s inside jokes, a shared cultural currency of humor, relatability, and randomness. The term itself comes from Richard Dawkins’ The Selfish Gene (1976), where it meant a cultural idea that spreads like wildfire. Fast-forward to today, memes are those images, videos, or phrases you see everywhere, from your group chat to your grandma’s Facebook (looking at you, Minion memes). They’re fun, quick, and endlessly remixable.

What makes a Meme?

A meme isn’t just a picture—it’s a mood, a vibe, a virtual wink. A good meme combines visuals (usually an iconic image or GIF), text (punchy captions that hit just right), and context (we all need to get it). The secret sauce? Relatability. Memes thrive on shared experiences and cultural moments, making us laugh because, let’s face it, we’ve all been there. Unlike a regular ol’ picture, a meme’s magic is its viral potential—it evolves, mutates, and spreads like internet wildfire, often getting better (or weirder) along the way.
